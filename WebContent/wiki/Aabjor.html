?bj?r is a small rural area in the eastern Norway region known as Valdres. It is a farming area ("bygd") that covers approximately 30 dairy farms. Estimated population is 200-250.

The area used to house a local school and general store, but these were closed down with the better public transportation and wider use of cars. Today the only bus that stops in the area is the bus to Gol from Fagernes, but it runs infrequently, and only has three stops in ?bj?r, none of which is in the center of the area.

?bj?r gets its name from the river ?bj?ra which runs through the area. The section of the river that passes through ?bj?r is known for its natural beauty, and has been named the local "Grand Canyon" by several books and tourist brochures. Some companies take tourists on guided walks down the river.

Attractions

The area is devoid of any historically interesting sights and is probably not an attraction in itself. However, the upper main road (E16) offers a wonderful view of the Nord-Aurdal valley. It is also possible to walk up to the top of the hillside from the main road to H?gesyn which offers an even better view. Many tourists take a break on their way from Gol to Fagernes or Oslo along the roadside to admire the view.

Apart from this, the river ?bj?ra is the main attraction. The Fagernes tourist office can help tourists get in touch with people and organizations arranging riverwalks.
