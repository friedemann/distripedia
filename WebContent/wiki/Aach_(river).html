The Aach is a river in Arbon in Switzerland, formed by the confluence of two rivers, each about nine kilometers long. It flows through the city of Arbon and after almost two kilometers flows into Lake Constance

The link road Arbon-Landquart crosses the Aach. Built in 1910, the bridge over the river was completely renovated for the first time in April 2006, after more than 90 years.[1]
